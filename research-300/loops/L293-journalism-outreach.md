## The thesis

Journalism outreach is OPENGEM's **highest-leverage discovery move** in the first 12 months. One FT or Reuters piece that cites OPENGEM is worth more than 10,000 organic users for the credibility cascade. But journalists have allergic reactions to anything that smells like PR — outreach must give them a *story angle*, not a press release.

The story angle that journalists *cannot resist*: "open-source startup publishes its own forecasting mistakes — Bloomberg can't." This is news because no incumbent does it.

## The pitch templates

>
> I'm Edgardo Muñoz Najar, building OPENGEM (opengem.org) — an open-source macro+geopolitics dashboard that publishes its full forecast track record, including the misses, on the same URL the original forecast lived on.

>
> Edgardo

**Template B — incident-driven** (when a real forecast misses badly):

>
> If the angle "what does it look like when a forecaster admits a miss in public?" interests you, the artifact is live now. No spin.
>
> Edgardo

**Template C — geopolitical event angle** (when a scenario triggers in real time):

> Hey [name],
>
> Our [Taiwan Strait / EU energy shock / etc.] scenario just triggered. The probability rolls up Bayesian-style from observable indicators — every trigger is machine-checkable. URL: [scenario page]. Methodology: [link].
>
> If you're working a piece on [topic], this is a numeric anchor with a citation chain. Use freely; attribution if you want it.

## The "no follow-up" discipline

We send the email once. We do not follow up. If the journalist is interested, they reply. If not, we move on. This eliminates the PR-grind feel that journalists hate.

## Avoiding the trap: don't pitch positive stories

We do **not** pitch "look how good our forecast is." That's PR. We **do** pitch:
- A miss + post-mortem (rare; high-novelty)
- A scenario trigger + decision-relevant numeric (moderate; tied to news cycle)
- The methodology / track-record asymmetry (one-time; the launch story)

This filter means we send maybe 20-30 pitches per year, not 200.
